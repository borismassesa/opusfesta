# Warm, direct, occasionally playful.

Write the way the best wedding planner in Dar es Salaam would speak — confident, practical, never showing off. Second person. Short sentences. Contractions are fine.

## Headline anatomy

1. **Eyebrow** (optional) — "For vendors", "Free to start. Always."
2. **2-3 line UPPERCASE display** with `<br/>` for rag.
3. **Optional lavender emphasis** on one word.
4. **15-25 word lead paragraph** below.
5. **One primary CTA + one text link.**

## CTA vocabulary

Always 2-4 words, imperative verb first.

- Start planning
- Find vendors
- Get started free
- Join as a vendor
- Browse ideas
- Explore tools
- See examples
- Talk to us

Never: "Click here", "Submit", "Continue".

## Reassurance lines

- "All in one place."
- "No credit card required · Set up in minutes"
- "Every vendor is reviewed and vetted. No surprises."
- "Messaging verified vendors is free, safe and secure."

## Locale cues — Tanzania first

- **Currency:** `TZS 35,000,000` — thousand separators, always, preceded by TZS.
- **Phone:** `+255 712 345 678` — spaced in 3/3/3 after the country code.
- **Cities:** Dar es Salaam, Zanzibar, Arusha, Moshi, Mwanza, Dodoma, Tanga, Morogoro.
- **Dates:** `December 14, 2025` — spelled month, no abbreviations in marketing. `Dec 14` only in dense UI.
- **Language:** English first, Swahili second where the user-facing surface is localised.

## Examples

> ✅ **"Your next client is Searching."**
> Eyebrow kicker, uppercase display, one-word lavender emphasis, period at the end.

> ❌ **"Let us help you find more clients for your business today!"**
> Too long, too salesy, no rhythm.

> ✅ **"Free to start. Always."**
> Promise + reassurance. Two short clauses.

> ❌ **"Try OpusFesta for free — no strings attached!!"**
> Exclamation marks, em-dash filler, "try" is weak.

## Do / Don't

<DosAndDonts>
  <Do>Write in second person: "you", "your", "yours". Never "users".</Do>
  <Dont>Use corporate filler: "leverage", "seamless", "robust".</Dont>
  <Do>Use contractions. "We&apos;re", "you&apos;ll", "can&apos;t" read warmer.</Do>
  <Dont>Stack exclamation marks. One per headline, at most — usually none.</Dont>
  <Do>Name the outcome, not the feature. "Plan without the chaos" beats "Advanced planning tools".</Do>
  <Dont>Use words like "solution" or "offering" when you mean "tool" or "service".</Dont>
</DosAndDonts>
